Welcome to Dungeon Dice

Embark on an adventurous journey through dungeons filled with dangers and treasures. Use your wits and dice to navigate through challenging scenarios and emerge victorious!

Getting Started

To play Dice Dungeon, you will need:

  • A set of standard six-sided dice (d6).
  • Each player will need one d6 dice and one four-sided dice (d4).
  • Dungeon tiles or a way to represent the dungeon layout. Please see the readme at HERE

How to Play!

Start by placing the first dungeon tile and positioning each player's character, represented by a d6, on the tile. Place one door on the tile to indicate an exit or entrance.
Determine order of the hero dice, this stays the same the entire game. That player can now take the turn
When a door is opened, click the draw card button the app.
When the tile is cleared or completed, roll a d6 if its an even number place 2 doors anywhere you like in the room if its an odd number place 1
Each player starts with 3 lives and a maximum health of 6.
Adjust the d6 to represent current health. When damaged by enemies or cards
Losing all health results in a loss of one life and restarting with full health.
If any player loses all lives, the scenario is restarted.

Player Turn

Each player can perform two actions per turn in any order. The actions include:

  • Move: Roll a d6 to determine how many spaces you can move. Move your character after the roll
  • Melee Attack: Attack an adjacent enemy by rolling a d6 for damage.
  • Ranged Attack: Attack an ememy dice one tile away or diagonally with d4 for damage. You can shoot through allies but not enemies
  • Heal: Roll a d4 to heal yourself, adjacent, or diagonally ally.

Enemies Turn

Enemies always go second in inititive. When you open a door the hero's will get to finish their current cycle of turns before the enemies turn.
Enemies on the board will move and attack during their turn.
Roll a 1d6 for the enemies to move Enemies target the closest player or the last player to attack them. In case of equal distance, the players decide who gets attacked.
Enemies roll a 1d4 and can only do malee attacks
